November 2021

Quietude with Claude Monet.

In the western corner of Jardin des Tuileries, a small line had formed outside the Musée de l'Orangerie. There was no doubt as to what everyone was here for; we shuffled our feet impatiently, rubbed our hands together for warmth in the November chill.

It was half past one when we finally entered the atria, and there was a quietude in the air. Curiously, one doesn't feel the crowds. When you're in the museum, it's almost as if you're in Monet's Giverny, seeing his water lilies through his eyes. Skylights flood the space in natural light, bathing the landscape in various sunrise and sunset hues.

The experience is simple and stripped of pretense; such is the pleasure of art.

Points of Interest

  1. Musée de l'Orangerie | Here, Monet's water lilies have replaced the Napoleon citrus trees as the space's most prized possession
    Jardin Tuileries, 75001 Paris, France.
  2. Musée Rodin | In Rodin's gardens, we spotted the odd pairs of lovers, standing shoulder blades kissing, postures more intertwined than his cheeky sculptures
    77 Rue de Varenne, 75007 Paris, France.
  3. Fondation Louis Vuitton | Frank Gehry's glass sailboat sits boldly within the 16th arrondissement of Paris, connecting the Jardin d'Acclimatation to the north, and the Bois de Boulogne to the south
    8 Av. du Mahatma Gandhi, 75016 Paris, France.
  4. Fondation Cartier pour l'art contemporain | Contemporary art in the midst of a modern woodland garden, a Jean Nouvel creation
    261 Bd Raspail, 75014 Paris, France.
  5. Villa La Roche | Le Corbusier's block-colored walls and right-angled architecture
    8-10 Sq. du Dr Blanche, 75016 Paris, France.
