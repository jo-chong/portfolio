Extraordinary Pleasures

August 2022

Maximize all the pleasure.

Punch was where I first met Xuan, cozied up against a corner straddling the border between the outdoor terrace and the bustling indoors. This gave us plenty to see (“see the creepers?”, he pointed out), to hear (with Toro’s Ordinary Pleasure beating gently in the background, just loud enough for us to catch the occasional lyric) and to smell (nothing turns heads faster than the smell of a freshly pulled shot or crispy glistening bacon). We definitely overstayed our welcome that afternoon, as diners came and went but we remained, the last of our iced coffee turning to a murky diluted version of its former golden brown self. Since then, we’ve been back a few times for their smashed avo and earl grey pancakes, and also just because; it’s a special place to us.

It goes without saying that brunch is my favourite mealtime, simply because my hospital schedule makes it so elusive. When these golden weekends present themselves, we pursue then with a vengeance. Good weather calls for sitting outdoors, whether seated on the steps outside Chū and Co, or on picnic mats at the beach. Now that we have added road cycling to our repertoire, cafés get brownie points if they're bike friendly.

On post-call days, we hit up our go-to indie coffee houses and "do a walkies". With an ice white in one hand and Xuan's (sometimes it's a pastry) in the other, there's really no better way to spend the afternoon.

Points of Interest

  1. PUNCH | If the weather's friendly, grab a seat at the outdoor patio. The light hits your brunch plate just right, it's impossible not to have a good time
    32 N Canal Rd, Singapore 059288.
  2. Bearded Bella | Some solid grub paired with solid koff
    8 Craig Rd, Singapore 089668.
  3. Chū and Co | Xuan and I cycle here for the best grilled cheese toastie in town
    15 Lichfield Rd, Singapore 556835.
  4. Double Up Coffee | Double Up has been my ride or die for years: my first iced white with Mura Masa playing softly in the background (vibe). A few to-gos paired with Motherdough’s almond croissant from down the street. This place has seen me through bleary-eyed post-call afternoons, heartbreaks and good conversations
    7 Jln Klapa, Singapore 199319.
  5. Mother Dough | See above-mentioned almond croissant (best in town, promise)
    3 Jln Kledek, Singapore 199259.
  6. Chye Seng Huat Hardware | Mom calls this spot endearingly by its Chinese name, 再成發. It's a metal/hardware shophouse turned 3-in-1 cafe, roastery and a tiny store, and makes me extremely glad I’m neighbours with the Jalan Besar district
    7 Jln Klapa, Singapore 199319.
  7. Woodlands Sourdough | I’ve been chasing Woodlands Sourdough for years, back when they were nestled in a corner at Serene Centre, to when they closed shop for a few years to travel and hunt sourdough, and finally to their current home along Upper Thomson. Their nut butter toast marked the start of my bread obsession and I've never loved another carb the same way since
    183 Upper Thomson Rd, #01-03, Singapore 574332.
  8. MICRO bakery & kitchen | My frequent date spot with Mom
    10 Jalan Serene #01-05 Serene Centre, 258748.
