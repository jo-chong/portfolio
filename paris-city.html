PARIS | FRANCE

A Moveable Feast

November 2021

Wherever you go for the rest of your life, it stays with you, for Paris is a movable feast.

Such is the splendor of Paris - an undeniable, indiscriminate charm felt by Hemingway and shared by countless travellers after him. It is beautiful at all hours, but the mornings are my favourite.

Before sunrise, it is still, with the occasional sound of delivery trucks pulling up at boulangeries breaking the silence. The air is crisp and cold, and we huddled under the covers.

7am is when the magic happens. Lights flicker on in the Haussmannian buildings surrounding our tiny apartment, golden rectangles framed by blue and yellow stone. We stayed in what was traditionally the chambre de bonne (maid’s room) - a mansard roof with attic rooms lit by dormer windows. The toilet was outside along the common corridor - drink too much before bedtime and you'll have to scurry out in the middle of the night, a roll of toilet paper in tow.

When we finally roll out of bed at 9am, our morning routine begins. Xuan toasts yesterday's leftover baguette/pita while I take intermittent bites between getting ready. If we're lucky, we'd have cheese picked up from a local shop to pair with. Our host had an eclectic taste in music, and we dutifully flipped through her entire vinyl collection. Some days, we hummed along to Penguin Cafe Orchestra, other days we vibed to Pink Floyd. Not quite the same as our usual Spotify playlists, but what's travelling if not to experience new things? After a quick run through of our checklist (Camera? Yup. Tripod? Yup. Wallet and keys? Oh shucks, where are they?) and 6 flights of stairs, we hit the streets running (read: panting).

Points of Interest

  1. Le Bon Goût Fermier | We stuffed ourselves silly at this charming cheese shop. The Tête de Moine, pared into rosettes right before us, made me giddy with excitement. A beautiful flower, with melt in your mouth texture. We popped by the Lecourbe outlet, where the Eiffel Tower can be spotted around the corner
    3 Rue Lecourbe, 75015 Paris, France.
  2. Jardin du Palais-Royal | Spend a slow afternoon strolling the gardens and their surrounding arcades, with a warm cuppa from Cafe Kitsuné next door
    43 Rue de Valois, 75001 Paris, France.
