SINGAPORE

Extraordinary Pleasures

August 2022

Maximize all the pleasure.

Punch was where I first met Xuan, cozied up against a corner straddling the border between the outdoor terrace and the bustling indoors. This gave us plenty to see (“see the creepers?”, he pointed out), to hear (with Toro’s Ordinary Pleasure beating gently in the background, just loud enough for us to catch the occasional lyric) and to smell (nothing turns heads faster than the smell of a freshly pulled shot or crispy glistening bacon). We definitely overstayed our welcome that afternoon, as diners came and went but we remained, the last of our iced coffee turning to a murky diluted version of its former golden brown self. Since then, we’ve been back a few times for their smashed avo and earl grey pancakes, and also just because; it’s a special place to us.

It goes without saying that brunch is my favourite mealtime, simply because my hospital schedule makes it so elusive. When these golden weekends present themselves, we pursue then with a vengeance. Good weather calls for sitting outdoors, whether seated on the steps outside Chū and Co, or on picnic mats at the beach. Now that we have added road cycling to our repertoire, cafés get brownie points if they're bike friendly.

On post-call days, we hit up our go-to indie coffee houses and "do a walkies". With an ice white in one hand and Xuan's (sometimes it's a pastry) in the other, there's really no better way to spend the afternoon.

Points of Interest

  1. Boulangerie Utopie | Pastry powerhouse
    20 Rue Jean-Pierre Timbaud, 75011 Paris, France.
  2. Mori Yoshida | Our best croissant (as the culinary mythology goes, the Japanese do it better)
    65 Av. de Breteuil, 75007 Paris, France.
  3. fringe | There is a quiet confidence about this space that attracts artists, photographers and travellers seeking a solid brew
    106 Rue de Turenne, 75003 Paris, France.
  4. Télescope Café | Cosy is the word one looks for when describing neighbourhood cafés. Owner Nicolas Clerc certainly gets it, and runs a charming coffee bar paving the way for Paris' third wave scene
    5 Rue Villédo, 75001 Paris, France.
  5. KB Café Shop | More than juste un café, KB provides inviting wooden tables, airy high ceilings and a hodgepodge of everything coffee-related. Xuan's favourite by far
    53 Av. Trudaine, 75009 Paris, France.
  6. Mokonuts | Middle-Eastern inflected lunch table
    5 Rue Saint-Bernard, 75011 Paris, France.
  7. Septime La Cave | The folks here know a thing or two about natural wine & dope evenings
    3 Rue Basfroi, 75011 Paris, France.
  8. Papilles Coffeehouse & Restaurant | Henceforth, every savoury pancake will be judged against, and most likely pale in comparison to, Papilles' glorious stack topped with crispy bacon and a sunny side up
    77 Rue Marguerite de Rochechouart, 75009 Paris, France.
  9. Naritake Ramen | Before our choice of cuisine raises any brows, have a try. When it's 10 degrees out, you'd be thanking us between slurps of their very rich, very fatty, pork belly broth
    31 Rue des Petits Champs, 75001 Paris, France.
  10. Crêperie Little Breizh | I'll let you in on a not so well-kept secret: visit this friendly Breton café during lunch and treat yourself to a buckwheat galette, a sweet crêpe and a glass of cider for 10 euros
    11 Rue Grégoire de Tours, 75006 Paris, France.
  11. Le Ruisseau - Burger Joint | They claim to be the best burger in town. We're not sure, because this was the only one we had, repeatedly
    22 Rue Rambuteau, 75003 Paris, France.
